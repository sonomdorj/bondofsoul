The story

A young man, left brain-damaged by a childhood fall from a racehorse, fights to keep the boy fate has placed in his care from being sent away to an orphanage. Tsoomoo's birth mother left when he was two months old; Balgaa has raised him ever since with a father's love.

The race

1975. The night before Naadam, the bay mare Sogoo Kheer eats dirt — a horse in peak form, in danger of going down on the course. To protect Tsevelmaa, the girl set to ride her, young Balgaa pulls her from the saddle and rides the race himself. Far ahead of the field, within sight of the Naadam grounds, horse and boy come crashing down.

Who has the right to love a child?

CHARACTER POSTERS

A father whose only argument is love. The boy who refuses to leave him. The mother who returns after twenty years. The grandmother whose passing sets everything in motion. And the childhood friends — once child jockeys together — now divided over one boy's fate.

From the director

"As a boy I was a jockey. I rode to win — then my body grew, and the racing ended before I was ready. This film is made from what stayed tethered to me since. Do not weigh a person by the outside. A true parent is not the one who gives birth, but the one who stays."

E. BATNYAMBUU — WRITER, DIRECTOR, BALGAA
